History In 1998, the owners of the Vickers concern decided to get rid of the assets of Rolls-Royce Motors. The most attractive buyer was the German automaker BMW, which already supplied engines and components for Rolls-Royce and Bentley vehicles, but BMW's final £ 340m offer was outbid by another German auto giant, Volkswagen, with a £ 430m offer. VW received from Vickers the rights to the Spirit of Ecstasy statuette and the signature Rolls-Royce radiator grille, the Bentley brand , the models , production and production facilities, headquarters. But VW was left without the Rolls-Royce RR trademark and logo because the engineering company Rolls-Royce plc, which owned the rights to use them, decided to keep them in the event of a sale to the automotive division. Rolls-Royce plc transferred its rights to the Rolls-Royce brand and logo to BMW for $ 40 million because the two firms had joint projects.
